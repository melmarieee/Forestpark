The park was established in 1993 after the government's education department offices were transferred to its present location in Pasig. Through a memorandum of agreement signed between the City of Manila and Winner Foundation supported by then First Lady Amelita Ramos, the city agreed to lease the site for development as a nature park by the private environmental group.

With an initial 150 century-old trees that survived the war, the park now hosts over 3,000 trees through reforestation efforts spearheaded by the Manila Seedling Bank.

Arroceros Urban Forest Park is often referred to as a "breathing space" in the midst of the urban environment of Manila. It serves as a green oasis where people can escape the hustle and bustle of the city and enjoy a tranquil environment filled with trees, plants, and wildlife.
