Trees

Trees are perennial plants with a woody stem or trunk, supporting branches and leaves. They play a crucial role in the environment by producing oxygen, absorbing carbon dioxide, providing habitat and food for various organisms, preventing soil erosion, and offering shade and aesthetic beauty.

African Tulip Tree

The flower bud is ampule-shaped and contains water. People sometimes play with these buds because of their ability to squirt water. The sap sometimes stains yellow on fingers and clothes. The open flowers are cup-shaped and hold rain and dew.

Mahogany Tree

Mahogany is wood from any of three tree species: Honduran or big-leaf mahogany, West Indian or Cuban mahogany, and Swietenia humilis. Honduran mahogany is the most widespread and the only genuine mahogany species commercially grown today.

Banyan Tree

A banyan, also spelled "banian", is a fig that develops accessory trunks from adventitious prop roots, allowing the tree to spread outwards indefinitely. This distinguishes banyans from other trees with a strangler habit that begin life as an epiphyte, a plant that grows on another plant.
